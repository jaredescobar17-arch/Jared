# The Alpha is not LLM monitoring — Kevin Indig (Growth Memo)

- **Author:** Kevin Indig
- **Source:** https://www.growth-memo.com/p/the-alpha-is-not-llm-monitoring
- **Published:** 2025-11-26 (sent to 22,913 subscribers)
- **Collected:** 2026-06-19 via web fetch (free portion; paid section omitted)
- **Thesis:** Exclusive data on the AI Search funding landscape — the durable value is
  in "Agentic SEO" tools that *ship work*, not in LLM-monitoring dashboards.

---

Adobe just paid $1.9 billion for Semrush — not for LLM tracking dashboards, but for the platform, customer relationships, and distribution. Contrast: investors poured $227M into AI visibility tracking, mostly tracking dashboards. The companies shipping outputs from Agentic SEO raised a third of that. Adobe's acquisition proves dashboards were never the point.

Analysis done with Wordlift, Growth Capital, Niccolo Sanarico, Primo Capital, and G2.

## 1/ AI Visibility monitoring looked like the future
Of 80 companies with $1.5B collective funding: Established platforms (5) captured $550M; LLM Monitoring (18) split $227M; Agentic SEO companies got $86M. Every CMO asked "How does my brand show up in ChatGPT?" but measuring isn't defensible — the sheer number of identical products proves it.

**Monitoring tools have negative switching costs. Agentic tools have high switching costs.**
- Low pain: turn off a monitoring dashboard, you lose historical charts.
- High pain: turn off an Agentic SEO platform, *your marketing stops publishing*.

The AI Visibility industry could be bigger than the SEO industry (~$75B) because Brand and Product Marketing care too. But Amplitude offered monitoring for free, and when Semrush added it as a checkbox, the category collapsed.

## 2/ The Alpha is in outcomes, not insights
In 2025, the value of AI is getting things done; monitoring is table stakes. 73% of AI-visibility companies were founded in 2024 and raised ~$12M on average — a check size usually reserved for proven product-market fit.
- Monitoring companies (avg age 1.3 yrs) raised seed capital at growth valuations.
- Agentic SEO companies (avg age 5.5 yrs) have built infrastructure for nearly a decade, yet raised a third as much.

VCs dislike "shipping" tools at seed (integration, approval workflows, human-in-the-loop looks like low-margin consulting). Monitoring looks like perfect SaaS (90% margins, instant onboarding). Money optimized for *ease of adoption* and missed *ease of cancellation*.

**Three capabilities separate winners from features:**
1. **Execution Velocity** — ship content across Reddit, TikTok, Quora, and search simultaneously; automate insight → publication.
2. **Grounding in Context** — systems that understand your specific business logic and brand voice ("ontology is the new moat").
3. **Operations at Scale** — pipeline management + governance across dozens of channels. "Point solutions lose; platform plays win."

The difference: one group solves "how do I know?", the other solves "how do I ship?"

## 3/ The next 18 months will wipe out the weakest part of the AI stack
- **Established platforms win by commoditizing.** Semrush/Ahrefs have 20-year relationships and already added LLM monitoring; risk is moving too slowly to the action layer. They're read-optimized; agentic ops require write-access to the customer's CMS.
- **Agentic SEO platforms scale into the gap.** Example: AirOps ($40M Series B, $225M valuation) lives in the action layer — content generation, maintenance, rich-media automation.
- **Monitoring tools consolidate or disappear.** Standalone AI-visibility vendors have ~18 months to build execution layers or find an acquirer. "Runway math" points to a Q3/Q4 2026 "Extinction Event" as 2024-hype rounds run dry → down-rounds, acqui-hires, or folds.

Andrea Volpini (Wordlift): "After 25 years, the Semantic Web has finally arrived… the human-centered web is turning into an agentic, reasoning web while most of the industry is caught off guard."

## 4/ Market thesis
SEO is a ~$166B opportunity split between tools (~$85B) and services (~$81B), growing 13%+ annually. Agencies hold 55% of services and 60% of enterprise spend flows to large consulting relationships — a $50B+ consulting layer sitting "directly in AI's disruption path." Content-focused SEO roles declined 28% in 2024 as AI automated routine work, while leadership/strategy roles grew 50–58%.

## 5/ What this means for you
- **Buyers:** Stop asking "Is it AI-powered?" Ask: Does it solve an operational constraint or just inform me? Does it automate a workflow or create new manual work? Could my existing platform bundle this in 18 months?
- **Investors:** The narrative layer (monitoring) is collapsing; the substance layer (execution) is underfunded; the gap closes fast. Ask "What would Semrush/Ahrefs need to do to provide this?" If "not much," it's not defensible.
- **Builders:** The question isn't which category to enter, but "How deeply integrated will I be in my customers' workflows?" If you're "just generating better content faster," you're a feature. If you're "managing complexity across dozens of channels while enforcing consistency," you're a platform.
